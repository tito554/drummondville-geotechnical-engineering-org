CPT (Cone Penetration Test) in Drummondville: Continuous Soil Profiling

Sound ground. Sound decisions.

LEARN MORE

Drummondville sits on the deep post-glacial sediments of the Saint-François River valley, where the Champlain Sea deposited thick sequences of sensitive silty clay interbedded with glacial till. The water table across Centre-du-Québec often sits within two meters of surface, and seasonal saturation cycles create challenging conditions for traditional drilling. These fine-grained lacustrine deposits demand continuous, high-resolution profiling to capture thin sand lenses, silt seams, and soft clay layers that standard SPT sampling can easily miss. Our CPT program applies ASTM D5778 methodology with a 15 cm² piezocone, recording tip resistance (qc), sleeve friction (fs), and dynamic pore pressure (u2) every centimetre through the column. For deeper investigations near the Saint-François or in the Saint-Nicéphore area, we combine CPT soundings with grain-size analysis to validate the friction-ratio soil classification against laboratory particle distribution curves, and with liquefaction assessment when loose saturated sands appear within the upper 10 metres of the stratigraphic column.

In Drummondville's Champlain Sea clays, a 0.3-metre silt seam missed by sampling can control the entire settlement timeline — CPT catches it every time.

Methodology and scope

A recent investigation near the Cégep de Drummondville campus required full stratigraphic control under a proposed four-storey institutional building on varved clay. The site showed a classic Centre-du-Québec sequence: a desiccated crust to 1.5 m, underlain by soft Champlain clay with undrained shear strength dropping below 25 kPa between depths of 4 and 9 metres. CPTu soundings identified a thin 0.3 m silt parting at 7.2 m that conventional split-spoon sampling had completely missed; this continuous drainage layer became the controlling element for consolidation settlement calculations. The test program included dissipation tests at five depths to measure horizontal coefficient of consolidation (ch) directly, producing t50 values that fed the staged loading plan. In Drummondville's sensitive clay environment, where remoulded strength can fall to 10% of peak, the zero-disturbance nature of CPT profiling matters — no sampling recovery issues, no borehole collapse in flowing sands, and a complete digital record that supports both conventional bearing capacity analysis and finite-element modelling of the soil-structure system. When site conditions demand verification of tip classification, we pair the CPT log with in-situ permeability testing to confirm drainage characteristics across identified pervious horizons.

Local considerations

The Saint-François River valley presents a specific CPT hazard: encountering glacial till lenses below 12 metres that can cause rapid refusal and potential cone damage. Drummondville's till matrix contains Precambrian shield erratics up to boulder size transported from the Laurentians, and hitting one at speed means a bent rod or damaged load cell. Our pre-investigation desktop review always checks water well records and existing borehole logs to map the till surface before mobilizing. A second concern specific to sensitive Champlain clays is excess pore pressure generation during penetration, which can temporarily reduce effective stress around the cone and skew interpreted undrained strength. We manage this by scheduling dissipation tests at critical depth horizons and correcting Su values using site-specific Nkt factors calibrated against field vane shear data from previous Drummondville campaigns. The City of Drummondville's geotechnical review process expects CPT results submitted alongside at least one validation borehole in complex stratigraphy — the combination of continuous CPT profiling with targeted sampling meets this requirement efficiently.

What is the difference between CPT and SPT for Drummondville clay sites?

CPT provides a continuous, centimetre-by-centimetre digital record of soil response without disturbing the material, while SPT delivers discrete samples at 1.5-metre intervals with significant disturbance. In Drummondville\'s sensitive Champlain clays, SPT recovery is often poor and the act of driving a split spoon remoulds the clay, destroying the structure that controls its behaviour. CPT measures in-situ properties without this disturbance, and the pore pressure sensor (u2) captures drainage conditions that SPT cannot detect. The trade-off is that CPT retrieves no physical sample; for projects requiring laboratory testing, we recommend pairing CPT soundings with at least one validation borehole or complementary test pits.

Can CPT be used for foundation design under the National Building Code of Canada?

Yes. NBCC 2020 Division B, Part 4, accepts in-situ testing methods including CPT for determining geotechnical design parameters. CPT-derived undrained shear strength (Su) from net cone resistance using calibrated Nkt factors is directly applicable for shallow foundation bearing capacity and pile shaft adhesion calculations under CSA A23.3. For deep foundations in Drummondville clay, CPT-based methods such as LCPC (Bustamante & Gianeselli) or ICP-05 provide unit shaft friction and end-bearing values accepted by most geotechnical reviewers in Québec. The critical requirement is that Nkt factors be validated against site-specific or regional reference tests; we maintain a calibration database from previous Drummondville projects to support this.
