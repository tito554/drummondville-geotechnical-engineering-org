Methodology and scope

Soil behavior varies markedly between the compact glacial till found beneath the industrial sectors near Autoroute 20 and the softer, compressible marine clays prevalent in the residential expansions east of the Saint-François River. A drained triaxial compression test per ASTM D7181-20 on a till sample from the Saint-Majorique area might yield an effective friction angle exceeding 32 degrees, while an undrained test on the sensitive Champlain Sea clay from the Drummondville-Sud district could reveal a remolded shear strength less than 20 kPa. These contrasts, captured under multi-stage loading in a triaxial cell, directly inform the retaining walls design and the allowable bearing pressures that structural engineers require. The test procedure involves saturating the specimen under backpressure, consolidating it to the in-situ stress state, and then shearing at a controlled strain rate slow enough to permit pore pressure equalization across the sample height — a critical step when testing low-permeability clay.

  • Consolidated-Undrained (CU) triaxial with pore pressure measurement for effective stress analysis
  • Consolidated-Drained (CD) triaxial for long-term slope and embankment stability
  • Unconsolidated-Undrained (UU) triaxial for rapid assessment of cohesive soil strength during construction

Local considerations

Drummondville sits within the seismically active Charlevoix-Kamouraska zone, and the Champlain Sea clays that blanket much of the city center are notoriously sensitive to cyclic loading. Undrained triaxial testing on these deposits frequently yields a pore pressure coefficient at failure (Af) approaching 0.8, signaling a marked tendency for positive pore pressure buildup under shear — a condition that drastically reduces the effective stress and can initiate progressive failure during an earthquake. The Saint-François River alluvial plain also contains interbedded silt lenses within the clay matrix; these layers, when not identified and tested separately in a multi-specimen triaxial program, can give falsely optimistic strength values. A rigorous triaxial testing protocol in Drummondville must therefore include careful sample extrusion, X-ray or CT verification of internal structure, and a consolidation phase that replicates the in-situ effective stress with precision, since over-consolidated crusts near the surface often mask the much weaker normally consolidated clay at depth.

Which triaxial test type is most appropriate for the Champlain Sea clay in Drummondville?

Consolidated-Undrained triaxial with pore pressure measurement per ASTM D4767 is the standard for Champlain Sea clay. This test captures the undrained shear strength and the effective stress parameters (c', φ') necessary to model the long-term consolidation and seismic response of these sensitive marine deposits.

How long does a triaxial test take from sample delivery to results?

A standard CU triaxial test with three confining pressures typically requires 7 to 10 working days. Consolidated-Drained tests on low-permeability clays may extend to 14 days due to the slow shearing rate required to maintain drained conditions. Expedited schedules are available for urgent foundation design deadlines.

What sample quality is required for reliable triaxial testing?

Undisturbed Shelby tube samples are essential for triaxial testing of the sensitive clays in Drummondville. The sample must be sealed immediately upon extrusion, stored upright at field moisture content, and transported without vibration. Disturbed or remolded samples yield strength parameters that are not representative of the in-situ formation.
